DAY 1: LUXOR

Luxor offers one of the most exquisite ancient treasures in the world. The Valley of Kings, the legend temple of Queen Hatshepsut, Karnak Temple are one of the many jewels to explore in the magic city of Luxor.

DAY 2: KARNAK - ESNA

Esna, a busy little farming town, located 55 km south of Luxor, on the west bank of the Nile. The Temple of Khnum, located 9 meter below street level, is one of the highlights in Esna. The lively tourist-oriented market in Esna is a perfect place to immerse in the real Egyptian vibes.

DAY 4: ESNA- EDFU

Watching the countryside with the little mud brick houses and observing the crops growing on the fertile land of the Nile, while sailing between Edfu and Esna, catapults you back in time. These paradise views are ideal to relax, leave your worries behind and become one with nature.

DAY 5: SILSILA

During the 18th dynasty the Egyptians started using sandstone instead of limestones. Gebel el-Silsila became an important stone source for several famous ancient Egyptians temples. Next to the extremely powerful energy and old vibes, Silsila is a very serene spot because regular boats can not park there.

DAY 6: KOM OMBO

Kom Ombo is a temple dedicated to the god Sobek (the crocodile god) and Haroeris (the winged god of medicine and one of the oldest incarnations of Horus). Crocodiles were worshipped at many cult-sites, from Qasr Qaroun in the Fayoum to Kom Ombo, and often buried with Pharaohs as it was believed that they would protect them in the afterlife. Kom Ombo temple is also known for its wall relieves showing ancient surgical and dental tools.
